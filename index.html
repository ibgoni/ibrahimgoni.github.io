About

I am a PhD candidate in Development Economics at the Centre d'Études et de Recherches sur le Développement International (CERDI), Université Clermont Auvergne (UCA), affiliated with CNRS and IRD. Raised in a farming family in Cameroon, I witnessed firsthand the structural challenges facing rural communities — this upbringing shaped my passion for designing data-driven, sustainable solutions.

My research explores the nexus of digital infrastructure, agricultural transformation, and ecological trade-offs in Sub-Saharan Africa. I study how digital technologies reshape rural economies, how agricultural systems adapt to environmental pressures, and the interactions between economic development and ecological sustainability.

I use causal inference methods — difference-in-differences, instrumental variables, and panel data econometrics — combined with large-scale household surveys (DHS, LSMS) and geospatial data to understand development dynamics in West and Central Africa.
