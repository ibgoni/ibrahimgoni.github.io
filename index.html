I am a PhD candidate at CERDI CNRS University of Clermont Auvergne. Raised in a farming family in Cameroon, I witnessed firsthand the structural challenges facing rural communities—this upbringing shaped my passion for designing data-driven, sustainable solutions.

My research explores the nexus of digital infrastructure, agricultural transformation, and ecological trade-offs in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Research in progress

  1. High Internet and Economic Development in Africa  (analysis & writing stage)
  2. Mobile Money and Agricultural Productivity: Evidence from Kenya  (design stage)
  3. Biomass Burning and Biodiversity Loss in Africa  (design stage)
  4. Public Policies to Internalize the Impacts of Agriculture on Biodiversity, with Marine Coinon, Céline Bonnet, Zohra Bouamra-Mechemache, and Clélia Sirami (writing stage)
